## Customer model

**Persona 1 — Linus, the used-car shopper in Göteborg.**
*Today (without this CLI):* Linus has three Bevakningar saved on Blocket — "Volvo XC70 under 80k SEK", "Subaru Outback under 100k", and a wider "stationsvagn 4WD" net. Every evening he opens blocket.se in two browser tabs, runs each saved search by hand, sorts by "Senaste", and eyeballs which thumbnails are new since yesterday. He copies a few interesting ad URLs into a Notes file with his guesses at fair price. He cannot answer "which of today's listings are priced below the median for this make+year+mileage band?" and he cannot tell at a glance which ads he already looked at last week.
*Weekly ritual:* Every evening, three saved-search refreshes plus 5-10 ad detail clicks; on weekends, a deeper "show me everything new in the last 7 days under 90k with under 20 000 mil" sweep.
*Frustration:* No way to see what is genuinely new versus what he scrolled past yesterday, and no way to flag the underpriced outliers without doing the math himself.

**Persona 2 — Sara, the apartment-furnishing deal-flipper in Stockholm.**
*Today (without this CLI):* Sara hunts BAP listings (sofas, ljudteknik, tools) priced below resale value — buys, cleans, resells. She runs a dozen ad-hoc free-text searches a day ("Genelec", "Eames", "B&O", "Festool"), sorted by "Lägsta pris" or "Senaste". She has no notion of historical pricing for what she's looking at; she relies on memory and a gut feel. She also tracks two specific dealer org_ids (a recurring estate-clearance company and a reseller who often dumps lots cheap). Today she clicks into each dealer's profile manually.
*Weekly ritual:* 30+ searches per week plus daily checks of two dealer pages.
*Frustration:* "Is this Genelec pair actually a deal or am I anchoring on the asking price?" — there is no median-price view per category+brand, and no way to be alerted when a watched dealer posts a new lot.

**Persona 3 — Erik, the dealership inventory analyst (small Mälardalen used-car lot).**
*Today (without this CLI):* Erik watches three competitor dealers by org_id and tracks how long their listings sit before re-pricing or disappearing. He clicks each dealer's Blocket profile every Monday, scrolls every page, and types the high-value listings into a spreadsheet — make, model, year, mileage, asking price, days listed. He cannot answer "which of competitor X's cars dropped in price this week" without remembering last week's prices.
*Weekly ritual:* Monday morning competitor sweep across 3-5 dealer org_ids; mid-week price-drop checks.
*Frustration:* No price history. Every Monday he is reconstructing last week's prices from memory or a stale spreadsheet, and stale listings (sat for 60+ days, still active) are invisible without manual bookkeeping.

**Persona 4 — Agent Margareta, an LLM coding agent driving the CLI on behalf of any of the above.**
*Today (without this CLI):* Margareta has no native Blocket tool. The one MCP that exists (`bjesus/begagnad-mcp`) is a thin search shim with no local state, no filter introspection, and no structured detail extraction. She cannot answer "what filters are valid for SEARCH_ID_CAR_USED" without a human pasting docs. She cannot pipe a search result into a downstream tool because outputs are markdown blobs.
*Weekly ritual:* Whenever a human asks her about a Swedish second-hand item, she falls back to web-fetching blocket.se HTML and parsing it.
*Frustration:* No JSON-out-by-default surface, no `--select` for nested fields, no filter discovery she can introspect — every query is a guess.

## Survivors and kills

### Survivors

| # | Feature | Command | Score | How It Works | Persona |
|---|---------|---------|-------|--------------|---------|
| 1 | What's new since timestamp | `since --search <name> --since <ts>` | 9/10 | Local SQLite query over `ads.timestamp` for a stored named-search WHERE clause; exits non-zero when rows match (cron-friendly). | Linus, Erik |
| 2 | Arbitrage / underpriced detector | `arbitrage --vertical car --make <m> --threshold 0.8` | 9/10 | Local SQL: compute median(price) per (make, model, year-band, mileage-band) from synced ads, list current ads where price ≤ threshold × median. | Sara, Linus |
| 3 | Price history per ad | `price-history <ad_id>` | 8/10 | Sync re-fetches tracked ads and writes `ad_price_snapshots(ad_id, ts, amount)`; command joins by `ad_id` and prints deltas. | Erik, Linus |
| 4 | Dealer portfolio + stats | `dealer ads <org_id> --stats` | 8/10 | Local aggregate over `ads` filtered by `org_id` joined with `ad_price_snapshots`: count, median price, oldest-listing-days, % price-dropped-this-week. | Erik, Sara |
| 5 | Stale listings | `stale --vertical <v> --older-than <Nd>` | 7/10 | Local SQL: ads where `now() - timestamp > N days` AND last sync still saw them as active. | Erik |
| 6 | Cross-vertical search | `search-all "<query>" --max-price <N>` | 8/10 | Local FTS5 UNION across the typed-vertical ad tables (BAP + car + boat + MC + 10 mobility verticals), merged and sorted. | Sara, Margareta |
| 7 | Watch with price-drop | `watch run <named-search> --notify-on price-drop` | 8/10 | Extends the absorbed `watch run` with a `--notify-on price-drop` mode that diffs current snapshots against the last run and exits non-zero with JSON listing dropped ads. | Linus, Erik, Sara |
| 8 | Description regex | `desc-grep --vertical <v> --pattern <re>` | 7/10 | Sync backfills `ads.description` by calling ad-detail for synced rows; command runs Go regexp over local descriptions. | Sara |
| 9 | Geo radius search | `geo near --lat <x> --lon <y> --radius <km>` | 7/10 | Local haversine over `ads.coordinates`; public search API only accepts Swedish region IDs. | Linus, Sara |
| 10 | Vehicle appraisal | `appraise --vertical car --make <m> --model <m> --year <y> --mileage <km>` | 7/10 | Local SQL: select comparable ads from synced corpus, compute p10/p50/p90 of price, render histogram. | Linus |

### Killed candidates

| Feature | Kill reason | Closest surviving sibling |
|---------|-------------|--------------------------|
| Filter discovery (`filters list`) | Already in absorb manifest #8 — would double-list | (absorbed feature #8) |
